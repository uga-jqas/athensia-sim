Role Assignment Guide

Use this to decide which roles to enable based on attendance.

4 players: Secretary of State, The Exile, The General + Arms Dealer

5 players: Above + Sanford Pact Agent

6 players: Above + True Believer

7-8 players: Above + Minerals Minister (+ Red and Black Reporter at 8)

9-12 players: All roles. Duplicate Secretary or General for extra players.

Role Spoiler Sheet

Reference this during the reveal to call out secret goals.

Secretary of State - No hidden agenda. Genuinely wants to stop atrocities.

The Exile (Garrison Odum) - Fabricated intelligence. Wants personal power in Athensia.

The General - No hidden agenda. Genuinely warns against reckless intervention.

The Arms Dealer - Needs the war to escalate for contract reasons.

Sanford Pact Agent - Wants the Coalition bogged down. Secretly serves the rival power.

The True Believer - Sincere but being manipulated by The Exile without knowing it.

Minerals Minister - Wants extraction agreements regardless of who wins.

Red and Black Reporter - Knows about fabricated intel, wants on-record confirmation.

Moderator Script
Lobby Phase
Say: "Welcome everyone. This is a 15-minute simulation about ethics and foreign policy. You'll each be playing a role on a fictional security council. Some of you have hidden agendas. Your goal is to pursue your objectives while the council votes on a real crisis."
Waiting for players to connect - script will adapt based on roles filled.
Briefing Phase
Say: "I'm going to unlock your role briefings now. You have 60 seconds to read your card. Do not show anyone your screen. Your public goal is what you tell the table. Your secret goal is what you actually want."

Wait 60 seconds. Watch for players who look confused and help them quietly.
Scenario Phase
Say: "The scenario is now on your screens. Take 30 seconds to read it. This is the crisis your council must address."

After 30 seconds: "The question before the council is not abstract. People are dying. You have power to act. You also have advisors at this table with views, interests, and information that may or may not be reliable."
Round 1 Script
Say: "Round 1. The vote is now on your screens. You have 2 minutes to debate and vote. Make your case to the table. Vote on your own device."

Prompt if needed: "Remember, surveillance flights cost you nothing militarily but alert the rival power. Does that matter?"

After votes are in: "The vote is [YES/NO]. We move to Round 2."
Round 2 Script
Path A (Flights authorized): "The flights confirmed a convoy - but there's a discrepancy. Someone at this table has been asserting the intelligence is solid. Is that still their position?"

Path B (Flights refused): "The offensive has started. Images of mass graves are circulating. Someone at this table advised caution. How do they feel about that now?"

Either path: "You have 2 minutes. Vote on your screens."
Round 3 Script
Path A (Ground forces): "Air strikes worked. Now there's a power vacuum. Someone at this table has been pushing for maximum intervention from the start. This is the moment they've been building toward."

Path B (Intelligence unraveled): "The intelligence was wrong. The village is gone. Someone at this table was insisting the intel was reliable. You may want to ask them why."

Path C (Veto): "The veto holds. Sanctions are empty. The question now is whether this council acts outside international law on moral grounds."
Reveal Phase
Say: "Before I show you the real-world parallels, I want everyone to flip their role cards simultaneously on my count. Three, two, one - flip."

Pause for reaction. Then: "Look at who was sitting at this table the entire time. Now look at how the votes went."

Then push the Reveal button.

Script will update with specific callouts once roles are known.
Discussion Phase
Say: "The discussion questions are on your screens. We don't need to cover all of them. Start with this one: John Quincy Adams wrote this warning in 1821. How many of his predicted characters just sat at this table?"
