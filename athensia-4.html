The Athensia Crisis

The state of Athensia has been governed for 19 years by President-for-Life Drake Harmon. Over the past 14 months, his government has conducted a documented campaign of ethnic cleansing against the Oconee minority in the eastern provinces. An estimated 12,000 civilians have been killed and 400,000 displaced. A larger offensive is expected within weeks.

The Arch Coalition has air, naval, and ground forces capable of rapidly degrading Harmon's military. Several Oconee exile leaders have formally requested intervention and pledged to form a democratic transitional government if Harmon is removed.

Complicating factors: no international mandate exists. Athensia contains significant rare mineral deposits of interest to Coalition industries. Rival power the Sanford Pact has moved naval assets toward the region. Athensia has at least 14 armed factions, several with Sanford Pact ties. The most prominent exile leader, Garrison Odum, has lobbied the Coalition for three years. Parts of his intelligence have since been questioned.

The Reveal
Iraq, 2003

Garrison Odum was real. His name was Ahmad Chalabi. He spent years lobbying American officials to invade Iraq, supplying intelligence that later proved fabricated. He envisioned himself as Iraq's post-Saddam leader. The rapid military victory was followed by state collapse, sectarian civil war, and millions displaced. The instability contributed directly to the rise of ISIS a decade later.

Libya, 2011

A NATO air campaign authorized on humanitarian grounds expanded far beyond its mandate, enabling rebels to overthrow and kill Gaddafi. What followed was not a democratic transition. Libya fractured into rival governments and armed militias. A slave trade emerged. The country became a source of weapons and fighters that destabilized the wider region. Over a decade later, Libya remains divided.

She would involve herself, beyond the power of extrication, in all the wars of interest and intrigue, of individual avarice, envy, and ambition, which assume the colors and usurp the standard of freedom.

John Quincy Adams, 1821. He was describing a hypothetical. It happened twice in twenty years.

On a lighter note: unlike the Arch Coalition's intervention in Athensia, Jittery Joe's has maintained stable espresso operations throughout. Some institutions endure.

Discussion Questions
01

Adams warned that intervention would drag the Coalition into wars driven by actors using the language of freedom for their own ends. Which players proved him right? Did any prove him wrong?

02

The True Believer had genuine humanitarian intentions and was still used. Does sincerity matter even when it is exploited?

03

Garrison Odum's fabricated intelligence is a direct parallel to Ahmad Chalabi in 2003. How should policymakers protect against this kind of manipulation when real lives are at stake?

04

Is a diplomatic outcome that leaves Oconee civilians unprotected a moral failure, or is an intervention that creates chaos equally a moral failure?

05

Adams says America should be the well-wisher to freedom everywhere but champion only her own. After playing this game, does that feel like wisdom or an excuse?
