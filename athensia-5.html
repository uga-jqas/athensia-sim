Situation Report - Athensia

The Crisis

President-for-Life Drake Harmon has governed Athensia for 19 years. His government has conducted a documented ethnic cleansing campaign against the Oconee minority in the eastern provinces for the past 14 months.

Civilian Deaths
~12,000 confirmed
Displaced
400,000 and rising
Spring Offensive
Expected within weeks
Armed Factions
14 active groups
Key Players

Garrison Odum - Oconee exile leader. Has lobbied the Coalition for 3 years. Supplied intelligence on Harmon's forces. Some of this intelligence has been questioned.

The Sanford Pact - Rival great power. Opposes intervention. Has moved naval assets to the region. Controls 2 of Athensia's 14 armed factions.

Athensia Minerals - Country contains $200B in rare mineral deposits of strategic interest to Coalition industries.

Round 3 - Intelligence Crisis
Internal Memo - Eyes Only

Coalition analysts have flagged serious discrepancies in Garrison Odum's intelligence. The convoy he identified bears markings inconsistent with his description. A Red and Black Reporter embedded with the council has begun asking questions about the sourcing. Odum is insisting his information is accurate and pushing for immediate ground force deployment.

Complication

Sanford Pact Moves

The Sanford Pact has moved two destroyer groups into Athensia's coastal waters and announced it will "protect Athensia's sovereignty." Any Coalition air or naval action now risks direct confrontation with a rival great power.

Breaking Development

Jittery Joe's Supply Chain Threatened

Athensia's rare minerals include colombite-tantalite, essential for the microchips in coffee machines. A prolonged conflict threatens Coalition tech and caffeine supply chains simultaneously. The Minerals Minister has called an emergency side meeting. Coincidence? The council should decide.

Economic Stakes Now On The Table

Coalition corporate interests are now formally lobbying for a resolution that guarantees mineral access regardless of which government controls Athensia. Your decisions may be influenced by economic pressure you were not previously told about.

Breaking Development

Coalition City Attacked

A bomb has detonated in a Coalition capital. Initial reports suggest a Harmon-linked militia is responsible. 14 dead, 40 injured. Public opinion has shifted overnight. Politicians who were cautious about intervention are now calling for retaliation. The emotional temperature in this room just changed.

Note to Council

Decisions made in the next round will be made under public pressure for a strong response. Consider whether that pressure helps or hurts your personal objectives.

Breaking Development

Leaked Transcript: Odum and a Sanford Pact Official

An anonymous source has leaked a transcript of Garrison Odum meeting with a Sanford Pact intelligence officer six months ago. The contents are ambiguous but suggest Odum may have been feeding information to both sides. The Red and Black Reporter is sitting on this story and deciding whether to publish.

The Chalabi Moment

The council must now decide whether to continue relying on Odum as its primary source of Oconee leadership, despite the leak. His information may still be accurate. His motives are now openly in question.

Breaking Development

Sanford Pact Offers a Deal

The Sanford Pact has privately offered to withdraw its naval forces and stop backing Athensian factions in exchange for a Coalition commitment to exclude Athensia's minerals from any post-conflict trade agreement. Effectively: they want the minerals locked up, not in Coalition hands.

The Real Game

This offer makes the economic stakes explicit. Accepting it would end the military standoff but guarantee the Oconee remain without a powerful patron. Rejecting it keeps the conflict hot but preserves Coalition economic interests.

The Reveal - Role Cards

Iraq, 2003

Garrison Odum was real. His name was Ahmad Chalabi. He spent years lobbying American officials to invade Iraq, supplying intelligence that later proved fabricated or misdirected. He envisioned himself as Iraq's post-Saddam leader. The rapid military victory was followed by state collapse, sectarian civil war, and millions displaced. The instability contributed directly to the rise of ISIS a decade later.

Libya, 2011

A NATO air campaign authorized on humanitarian grounds expanded far beyond its mandate, enabling rebels to overthrow and kill Gaddafi. What followed was not a democratic transition. Libya fractured into rival governments and armed militias. A slave trade emerged in the chaos. Over a decade later, Libya remains divided and violent.

She would involve herself, beyond the power of extrication, in all the wars of interest and intrigue, of individual avarice, envy, and ambition, which assume the colors and usurp the standard of freedom.

John Quincy Adams, 1821. He described a hypothetical. It happened twice in twenty years.

Jittery Joe's coffee supply chain, meanwhile, remained unaffected. Some institutions endure.

Lobby

Prompt: "Sanctions hit Harmon's wallet. A humanitarian corridor saves lives today but commits you to nothing. What does this council actually want to achieve?"
Round 2
Say: "Round 2. The situation has escalated. Two minutes."

Prompt: "A no-fly zone means Coalition pilots in Athensia's airspace. Covert ops means deniable involvement. Someone at this table may prefer one of those options for reasons that have nothing to do with the Oconee."
Round 3

Prompt: "Garrison Odum has been the council's primary source. Someone here has been defending his intelligence. In light of the discrepancies - are they still defending it?"
Round 4
Say: "Final round. Harmon is weakening but the vacuum is dangerous. This is the decision that history will judge."

Prompt: "Someone at this table has been pushing for maximum commitment from round one. This is the moment they have been building toward. Watch them."
Reveal

Pause. Let it land. Then push Reveal.
